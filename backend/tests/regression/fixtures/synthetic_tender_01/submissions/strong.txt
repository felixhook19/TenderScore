Greenhollow Grounds Limited — Tender for Grounds Maintenance Services SDC-2026-GM-014

Question Q1
Our mobilisation plan covers the first ninety days in three phases, each with a named lead. Phase one, led by our contracts director Hannah Pryce, completes TUPE consultation, equipment commissioning and site familiarisation visits to every park and verge section within thirty days. Phase two embeds the working schedules for both lots, including the seasonal uplift for the growing season from March to October, with cutting frequencies agreed against the specification and published to the council portal. Phase three completes quality baselining: every site receives a recorded condition survey, photographed and indexed against the schedule of rates. Our contingency arrangements include a reciprocal plant-hire agreement with our depot in the neighbouring district, generator-backed welfare units, and a severe weather protocol that redeploys crews to clearance work within four hours of an amber warning. We will hold weekly mobilisation reviews with the council's contract manager and publish an exceptions log, so any slippage is visible and corrected within the same week.

Question Q2.1
We will deploy twenty-two full-time equivalent operatives across the two lots, structured as four area teams each led by a working chargehand, with staffing levels flexed by published seasonal schedules rather than ad hoc overtime. Every operative holds or is enrolled for Lantra awards appropriate to their duties, and all spraying operatives hold PA1 and PA6 certification, refreshed on a three-year cycle and recorded in our training matrix. The training matrix is reviewed monthly by our operations manager and audited annually. We fund two horticultural apprenticeships per year through the contract, recruited locally.

Question Q2.2
Each area team is supervised by a dedicated contracts supervisor who completes documented site inspections every week. Supervisors hold SMSTS certification and carry tablet-based inspection software so findings are logged with photographs at the point of observation. Welfare provision includes heated cabins at both depots, mobile welfare units for remote verge work, and a fatigue management standard that caps seasonal overtime. Our employee assistance programme is available to all staff from day one.

Question Q3.1
Our quality management system is certified to ISO 9001:2015 and the certificate scope includes grounds maintenance operations. Every scheduled visit generates a digital completion record with geotagged photographs. Defects identified by our supervisors or by the council are logged in a single rectification register with a five working day closure standard; rectification visits are free of charge. The register is shared with the council in real time, and closure evidence is photographic in every case.

Question Q3.2
We will report performance monthly against the contract key performance indicators, with an open-book dashboard covering completion rates, rectification closure times, complaints and biodiversity measures. The dashboard is available to the council continuously, not only at review meetings. Quarterly contract reviews will include trend analysis and an improvement plan, and we propose an annual resident satisfaction survey delivered jointly with the council at our cost.

Question Q4
We will eliminate routine glyphosate use on amenity areas by the end of year one, replacing it with hot-foam treatment and mechanical removal, and we will maintain a published pesticide register for the exceptional cases where the council approves targeted use. Pollinator habitat is protected through a differential mowing plan that converts agreed verge sections to two-cut conservation regimes, with arisings collected. Our fleet plan replaces diesel mowers with battery-electric equivalents on a rolling programme reaching sixty per cent of the fleet by year three, and both depots already run on certified renewable electricity.

Question Q5
We will deliver two local apprenticeships per year, guaranteed interviews for care leavers and veterans, and a schools programme offering pollinator workshops to every primary school in the district. Ten per cent of contract spend is committed to local suppliers within the district boundary, reported quarterly. We will also provide one volunteer day per employee per year in support of the council's friends-of-parks groups.

Question Q6
Safety is managed under our ISO 45001-certified system. Safe systems of work for operations beside live carriageways follow Chapter 8 of the Traffic Signs Manual; every verge crew includes a trained traffic management operative, and task-specific risk assessments are reviewed before each season. We operate a near-miss reporting scheme with a monthly safety forum, and our RIDDOR procedure assigns reporting to a named competent person with a twenty-four hour internal escalation standard. Our three-year RIDDOR record shows no reportable incidents, and we will share safety performance data with the council quarterly.
