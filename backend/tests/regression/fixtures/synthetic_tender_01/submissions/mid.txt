Fenwick & Marsh Landscapes Limited — Tender Response SDC-2026-GM-014

Question Q1
We will mobilise the contract over ninety days using our standard mobilisation checklist, overseen by our regional manager. TUPE consultation will be completed in the first month, and equipment will be transferred from our existing contracts as they conclude. Service delivery schedules for both lots will be drafted during mobilisation and agreed with the council before the service commencement date, including higher cutting frequencies in the growing season. For severe weather we maintain a call-off arrangement with a local plant hire company and our crews can be redirected to priority clearance at the council's request.

Question Q2.1
We expect to employ around eighteen operatives across the two lots, organised into three teams. Team leaders are experienced grounds staff and all operatives receive toolbox talks and manufacturer training on the equipment they use. Spraying is carried out by our two certificated operatives who hold PA1 and PA6. Training records are held at head office and can be made available to the council on request.

Question Q2.2
Supervision is provided by a contracts manager who visits sites on a rota and carries out recorded inspections each month. Operatives have access to welfare facilities at our depot and portable facilities are provided for remote working. We comply with working time requirements and monitor overtime informally during the peak season.

Question Q3.1
We operate a documented quality system based on the principles of ISO 9001, though we are not currently certificated. Completed work is signed off by team leaders and spot-checked by the contracts manager. Defects reported by the council are rectified within ten working days and recorded in our maintenance log.

Question Q3.2
We will attend quarterly contract review meetings and provide a written performance summary covering completed schedules and any complaints received. Key performance indicator reporting will follow the council's template. We are open to developing additional reporting if the council requires it during the contract.

Question Q4
We will reduce pesticide use by adopting targeted spot treatment rather than blanket spraying, and we are trialling a hot-foam unit on another contract which we would extend to this council if the trial is successful. We support conservation mowing where the council designates suitable verges. Our fleet is maintained to manufacturer standards and we intend to introduce electric hand tools as existing equipment reaches end of life.

Question Q5
We employ locally where possible and would welcome the opportunity to take on an apprentice during the contract term. We sponsor a community football team in the district and our staff take part in an annual charity litter pick.

Question Q6
We hold a current health and safety policy and our risk assessments and method statements cover roadside working in line with Chapter 8 requirements. Crews working on highway verges set out signing and guarding before work begins. Accidents are recorded in the site accident book and our office manager submits RIDDOR reports where required. We have had one reportable incident in the last three years, which was investigated and closed with additional training for the crew involved.
